Lumen Health
A patient-facing post-visit companion that turns the clinical note into language patients can act on.

The Problem

Most patients forget the majority of what was said within minutes of leaving a consultation. The clinical note exists, but it is written for clinicians, not for the person who has to follow it. The gap sits between the scribe that captures the visit and the messaging that follows up.

Lumen Health is the consumer surface in that gap: a thought experiment on what could sit between a clinical scribe (like Heidi Scribe) and patient messaging (like Heidi Comms).

What I Built
  • Translates the clinical note into plain language, with every medical term tappable for a definition
  • Action items the patient can check off, with daily tasks tracked as a weekly streak
  • Medication instructions written to actually be followed, not decoded, with refill requests routed to a pharmacy
  • Lab results grouped by visit, each with a reference range and a colour-coded flag
  • Secure messaging that carries the visit context straight into a pre-drafted note to the right clinician
The Forward Deployed Angle

This is the muscle the role needs, built on my own time: define the customer surface area, ship it, instrument it, prove the loop. I started from a real clinical problem, designed the workflow a patient would actually use, and got it shipped and working rather than stopping at a mockup. The same pattern, understand the data, map it to a workflow, ship it for a specific user, handle the edge cases, is exactly what enterprise onboarding of a clinical AI product demands in the field.

Stack: Next.js 16 (App Router), React 19, TypeScript, shadcn/ui, Tailwind v4, Vercel
