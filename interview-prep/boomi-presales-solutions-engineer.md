## Recruiter Screen — Focus Round

### Format (expect this)

- 30 min phone or Teams call with an ANZ-based recruiter (likely Sydney APJ TA team)
- They are screening *for* the hiring manager, not deciding alone. Their job is to confirm: background fits JD on paper, comp expectations align, you are visa-ready and locally based, motivation is real (not spray-and-pray), and you communicate clearly under light pressure.
- They will **walk you through the rest of the process** at the end. Have questions ready.

### What they will ask (high probability)

| # | Question | Why they ask | Your angle |
|---|----------|--------------|------------|
| 1 | *"Walk me through your background."* | Confirms you can summarise crisply — SE skill test in disguise. | 90 seconds, chronological: Phocas BI consulting → IT Visions → UbiPark → inforcer pre-sales → now. Land on **"100+ technical deep dives, demos, POCs, and commercial close engagements"** verbatim. |
| 2 | *"Why Boomi? Why now?"* | Filters genuine interest from job-board blast. | Three beats: (a) iPaaS category leader, 11-yr Gartner MQ Leader, post-IPO; (b) AgentStudio + Rivery = the agentic data activation story is exactly where the market is going (Steve Lucas: *"2026 will be the year organisations stop experimenting with AI and start activating it at scale"*); (c) ANZ team is in growth mode and I'm Melbourne-based with the right visa, right archetype, right ramp profile. |
| 3 | *"What are you looking for in your next role?"* | Comp + motivation calibration. | "Primary archetype is Pre-Sales / Solutions Engineer at a category-leading platform with an AI-forward roadmap. Boomi hits all three — that's why this is at the top of my pipeline." |
| 4 | *"Tell me about your iPaaS / Boomi experience."* | The known gap from the JD. | Honest, forward-leaning: "No Boomi production experience. I've delivered enterprise integrations on REST APIs, SSO/SAML/OAuth/SCIM, and ERP middleware (MYOB) at three companies. I'm spinning up a free Boomi trial this week to build a working process before our next conversation. Two recent SaaS products I installed end-to-end using their own AI setup tooling in the last month — the ramp pattern is well-rehearsed." |
| 5 | *"What's your comp expectation?"* | The single most important answer of this call. | See **Comp Script** below. Do NOT undershoot. |
| 6 | *"Are you authorised to work in Australia?"* | Compliance gate. | "Australian citizen, no sponsorship required." |
| 7 | *"Are you based in Sydney or Melbourne?"* | Location gate. | "Melbourne-based. Happy to travel to Sydney for customer / team work as needed." |
| 8 | *"Are you actively interviewing elsewhere?"* | Urgency / leverage signal. | "Yes, currently in process with [Pendo / Heidi] for similar SE roles. Boomi is at the top of the list because of the AgentStudio + ANZ growth story." (Only say this if true; honesty + light leverage is fine.) |
| 9 | *"Notice period / when could you start?"* | Pipeline planning. | "Available within 2-4 weeks." |
| 10 | *"Any questions for me?"* | They expect 2-3. See **Questions to Ask** below. |

### Comp Script (rehearse this verbatim)

> *"My target is around **AUD 220K OTE**, with flexibility on the base/variable split — I'd expect something like base **155-175K plus 50-70K variable** for a Mid-Senior pre-sales seat. Given Boomi is post-IPO, equity is also a meaningful part of the package for me. Can you share the band for this role and the typical on-target attainment for the ANZ SC team?"*

Why this works: anchors at the **mid of the AU SE band** (RepVue shows AU SE OTE A$170K-A$288K; Boomi US PSE listed $140-175K base on Glassdoor, ANZ should land at the upper-mid of AU benchmark for a Mid-Senior seat). Asking back about the band and attainment rate is what a real SE does in discovery — they will notice.

### Questions to Ask (pick 2-3, do not ask all)

1. *"Steve Lucas said 2026 is the year orgs stop experimenting with AI and start activating it. How is the ANZ SC team being asked to lead that conversation with customers right now — is it AgentStudio-led, Rivery-led, or core iPaaS-led depending on the account?"* (proves you read recent comms, opens a strategy convo)
2. *"How is the SC team structured in ANZ — are SCs aligned 1:1 with AEs, by industry, or pooled? And what's the ramp expectation in months 1-3?"* (operational, signals you've done SE before)
3. *"What does success look like at the 6-month mark for this seat — is it technical-win count, attainment, or something else?"* (forward-looking, lets you reverse-engineer the scorecard)
4. *"What's the most common reason candidates don't make it through this loop?"* (rare to ask, recruiters appreciate it — gives you the failure modes to avoid)

Avoid: salary deep-dive beyond the script (save for HM / offer stage), competitor bashing, anything that sounds like you're shopping offers casually.

## Company Signals — What to Say & Avoid

### Vocabulary Boomi uses (use it back)
- **"Data activation"** (not "data integration" alone) — Steve Lucas's central narrative
- **"Agentic enterprise"** and **"control plane for agents"** — AgentStudio framing
- **"Intelligent integration and automation"** — Boomi's official descriptor
- **"Connection layer to control layer"** — Constellation Research framing of recent moves
- **iPaaS, ETL/CDC, MFT, API Management** — standard category vocab

### Values they screen for
- **Customer-obsession** — Boomi's careers content leans heavily into customer outcome stories
- **Technical credibility + business translation** — the JD is explicit
- **Self-starter under ambiguity** — repeated in Glassdoor reviews
- **AI-forward but pragmatic** — they want SEs who can demo AgentStudio without overpromising

### Things to avoid
- Don't bash MuleSoft or Workato by name. Frame as architectural difference (independent vs locked-in), not "we're better."
- Don't bluff Boomi product knowledge. Recruiters relay this to HMs and it kills credibility.
- Don't anchor low on comp. Boomi is post-IPO, well-funded, and ANZ is growth-mode.
- Don't ask about WFH / time off / perks on the recruiter call. Save for offer stage.
