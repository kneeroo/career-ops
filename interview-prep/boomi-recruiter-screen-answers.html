Opening posture — first 30 seconds

How you sound when they say hello sets the tone for the whole call.

"Thanks for making the time — really appreciate it. I've been following Boomi closely since the IPO and especially the AgentStudio launch, so I'm genuinely looking forward to this conversation. Keen to hear what you're looking for and where this seat sits in the ANZ team."

Why it works: confident, specific, signals homework in 15 seconds, hands the mic back. Don't apologise for anything, don't over-thank.

Q5 "What's your salary expectation?"

Single most important moment of the call. Flip it back first — never anchor before they do. If you go first, you cap yourself.

The strategy: Boomi has a budgeted band for this seat. Your job is to get them to share it first. If their band is higher than your number, you've left money on the table; if it's lower, you've disqualified yourself. Make them go first.

Step 1 — Flip it back (use this verbatim, pick one)

Option A — soft and warm:
"I'm open. My first priority is making sure this is a great fit on both sides. But if you've got a range you're able to share, I'd be happy to discuss against that."
Option B — more deliberate, signals package literacy:
"I know every organisation structures comp differently across base, variable, equity and super. With that in mind, can you help me understand what the package looks like for this seat and the range the team has budgeted? That'll let me give you a much sharper answer."

ANZ note: mentioning super (superannuation — 12% guaranteed) signals you're a local who understands the package isn't just base + variable. Recruiters react well to that.

Step 2a — If they share the band

If their band is at or above your target:
"Great — that aligns with what I was expecting for a Mid-Senior pre-sales seat in ANZ."
If their band is slightly low (or you want headroom):
"Thanks for sharing — that's really helpful. My expectations were initially slightly above the high end of that range, but I'm flexible for the right seat. Worth flagging now so we're aligned later, rather than at offer stage."

Why this works: you've banked their number, signalled you're worth the top of band, and kept the conversation moving without breaking rapport.

Step 2b — If they insist you go first

Recommended (market-anchored, in demand, ANZ-framed):
"My priority is making sure this is a strong two-way fit, and comp is one piece of that — base, variable split, equity, super all matter. From the conversations I'm in for similar Mid-Senior pre-sales seats in ANZ, the market is sitting around AUD 200 to 240K OTE, typically on a 75/25 base/variable split. How does that align with what Boomi has budgeted for this role?"
Shorter fallback if they really push for a single number:
"I'm currently in process for roles in the AUD 200 to 240K OTE range. Where does that sit relative to your band?"

Why this works: framing it as "the market is paying me X" instead of "I want X" makes it a fact, not a hope. The "how does that align" close immediately flips the question back so they still tell you their band before you commit.

Anchor data (for your reference, not to quote)

RepVue AU Sales Engineer OTE band: A$170K – A$288K. Boomi US PSE listed base: US$140–175K. A Mid-Senior ANZ SC at a post-IPO category leader should land A$200–240K OTE all-in, plus super and equity.

What "in demand" signals

Mentioning that you're "in process for similar roles" without naming the band as your ask = you're not hoping, you're priced. Recruiters move you up the queue when this is convincing.

Do NOT: volunteer a single number first ("I want 220K"). Do NOT say "I'm flexible" with no follow-up — that's permission to lowball. Do NOT quote USD numbers. Do NOT mention current/last salary if asked — in Victoria it's lawful to ask but you don't have to answer; deflect with "I'd rather anchor on the market rate for this role than on what I happened to be paid previously."
Mental model: the recruiter is not your adversary — they want to advance you. But every $10K higher you can credibly hold is $10K they'll need to justify to the HM. Make them work for the number, don't hand it to them.

Why: rare ask, recruiters appreciate it, gives you the failure modes to avoid in later rounds.

ANZ-Specific Notes

What's different about an ANZ recruiter call

  • Vocabulary: use "uni" not "college", "CBD" not "downtown", "AE" or "account manager" not "salesperson", "Telstra / NAB / BHP / Qantas / Coles" as the canonical enterprise reference accounts.
  • Comp in AUD only. Never quote USD numbers. The AU market sits below US Boomi bands and recruiters know the conversion games.
  • Public sector matters. Boomi has IRAP / Federal eligibility chatter. If the recruiter mentions gov accounts (Services NSW, ATO, DTA, federal agencies), you should know it's a real ANZ pillar.
  • Travel reality: ANZ SE patches usually mean Melbourne ↔ Sydney monthly, occasional Brisbane / Auckland / Perth. Signal you're fine with that.
  • MuleSoft is the elephant. ANZ is a strong MuleSoft market via Salesforce. Don't bash it. Frame as "independent control plane" vs "Salesforce-locked".
  • Boomi ANZ HQ: Sydney (North Sydney / CBD historically). Melbourne is a satellite. Be clear you're not asking for a Melbourne office.
  • Time zones: Boomi global HQ is Chesterbrook PA (US Eastern). Some calls will be early-morning AEST. Don't flag this as a problem.
  • Tall poppy reality: the 90-second narrative needs the "100+ POCs" number, but say it as fact, not boast. ANZ recruiters react badly to American-style chest-puffing.

The closing line

When they wrap up and walk you through next steps.

"Really enjoyed this — thanks for the context on the ANZ team. To recap on my side: Melbourne-based citizen, primary archetype match, OTE expectation around 220 AUD, and I'm spinning up a Boomi trial this week so I can speak to the platform concretely when I meet the hiring manager.
